About Me

I’m a student at Leyte Normal University and I’m currently immersed in web development and system design. I like merging logic and creativity, especially on projects that aim to make a real difference. Whether I’m crafting backend services or adding subtle UI animations, I tackle every problem in a structured way.

One practice I depend on is writing pseudocode. It lets me outline ideas in plain language before I touch real code, and it has become a cornerstone of my problem solving routine. I’ve always been curious about what runs beneath the surface even at the system level and I find purpose in building tools that empower communities.

Right now I’m developing EcoTac, a Progressive Web App to boost environmental engagement, alongside ShopTech, a secure e-commerce system with dynamic cart and authentication features. These projects have trained me to adapt while remaining detail oriented, weighing performance, user experience, and a design that stays true to the mission.

Featured Projects

Shop Tech System

🛒 ShopTech System — Built for Real Business Needs ShopTech is a custom backend e-commerce solution I developed to power my tech retail business, specializing in laptops and computer components. Designed with performance and reliability in mind, it includes secure user authentication, dynamic cart management, and straightforward inventory handling. The system is built using PHP, SQL, and JavaScript, ensuring compatibility with low-resource environments while remaining scalable for future growth. Every part of ShopTech was written from scratch to give me full control over how my products are managed, displayed, and sold. It’s more than code it’s the operational backbone for my business. Whether it's tracking laptop inventory, handling customer accounts, or optimizing system behavior for longer battery life and smoother use, ShopTech reflects my commitment to digital craftsmanship and practical commerce.

EcoTac System

🌱 EcoTac System — Technology for a Cleaner Tacloban EcoTac is a purpose-driven web application I developed to support sustainable practices in my hometown, Tacloban City. The system enables every barangay to update and document the condition of their local trash binspromoting proper segregation, cleanliness, and accountability. EcoTac includes a document upload feature, allowing barangays to submit proof and records of their waste management efforts directly through the platform. To encourage consistent participation, EcoTac introduces a reward system where the cleanest and most organized barangay's receive recognition for their efforts. Built with community in mind, this project reflects my commitment to using technology not just as a tool—but as a way to inspire civic pride, environmental responsibility, and collective progress.
